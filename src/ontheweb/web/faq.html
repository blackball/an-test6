Welcome Alpha Users

We are very excited that you are helping us test our service.
Below are some answers to commonly asked questions, which we'll try to update during the alpha testing period.
Q: What kinds of images will solve?
A: We don't know! That's why you are testing the service. What we do know is that we have solved images that range in size from under 10 arcmin to over 80 deg. We also know that we have solved images taken in bandpasses ranging from the near ultraviolet to the near infrared.
Q: I submitted an image and it didn't solve even though it had an almost exactly correct WCS header to begin. How can you guys suck so badly that you can't even solve an image when I give you the right answer?
A: Currently, astrometry.net ignores any existing image meta-data about pointing or rotation. It only does purely blind astrometry. Eventually we plan to perform refinement only WCS services in addition to the blind service. The current astrometry.net alpha service may use the input image WCS meta-data, if it exists, to guess the image scale, but beyond that all meta-data in the image are ignored. That is the magic, and principal limitation, of the service.
Q: Can I do precision proper-motion analyses with your output?
A: Right now we don't do extremely precise, proper-motion-grade astrometric solutions; these require performing simultaneous analyses of multiple images from the same instrument and simultaneous analyses of multiple images of the same part of the sky. This is on the long-term to-do list, but it ain't happening during the alpha phase. If you want precision, multi-image astrometry, we currently recommend taking the output of astrometry.net and using it as the input for SCAMP.
Q: Can I use your output to build panoramic or mosaic images from many individual images?
A: Yes! However, see our answer to the previous question: We are not performing the most precise possible measurements of camera distortions; if you want awesome mosaics, use Bertin's SCAMP and then its sister software, SWarp. Mosaic-quality WCS and automatic mosaicing of input data are both on our medium-term to-do lists.
Q: My image didn't solve even though I put in a correct estimate of the scale and I can see that your source extraction correctly extracted many USNO-B stars.
A: Can you phrase that as a question? We find that the most common error is to put in the scale as 25 but then accidentally pull down the tab size of the image in degrees when you meant size of the image in arcmin. If you are sure that you have entered everything correctly and that source extraction is sane, and it still isn't solving, send email to alpha@astrometry.net with your jobid, because you clearly have a useful image for debugging.
Q: My HST ACS images won't solve! Can't you even solve a perfectly calibrated and highly recognizable piece of astronomical data?
A: Right now there is a strict limitation that we cannot possibly solve any image below about 5 arcmin on a side. Don't even bother trying. We hope to overcome this eventually, but don't hold your breath!
Q: Why does my submission die silently without returning a jobid?
A: Did your job exceed the 250M size limit? If so, try gzip (yes, we take gz files). If not, there is a bug; send email to alpha@astrometry.net.
Q: What does the system do with multi-extension FITS files?
A: It just looks at the first image-like FITS extension.
Q: I have tons of images I want to solve. Typing my email and the size parameters and the image urls into your lame web form over and over again is driving me crazy. Aren't you guys real scientists? Can't you give me a high-throughput option?
A: In the future there will be a command line client as well as the option for you to install the entire system locally at your site. For now, you can speed things up by submitting your first image manually using the web form and then hitting the LinkToTheseParameters button. That will construct a url which pre-fills the form. From then on, all you'll need to change is the url or local filename of the image. If that's not good enough, the best we can offer you for now is the following hack, which uses wget (which you could have just copied from the LinkToTheseParameters link): wget "http://live.astrometry.net/index.php?justjobid&email=user@domain.edu&uname=YourName&xysrc=url&imgurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.astro.domain.edu%2Fuser%2Foutbox%2Fmyfield.jpg&fstype=ev&fse=1&fsv=50&fsunit=degreewidth&fsu=&fsl=&x_col=X&y_col=Y&parity=2&poserr=1&tweak=1&tweak_order=2&index=auto&submit=Submit" -Ojobid_out If that's not good enough, alpha tester Phil Marshall (UCSB) has written a csh script for uploading multiple files you can grab here.
Q: I looked at the source extraction preview image on the status page for one of my jobs, and your system totally failed to find tons of perfectly obvious sources in my image. What's up?
A: We're using a really *dumbass* source extraction algorithm for now, since it basically has to Just Work (tm) most of the time. We'd like to make it more sophisticated, so please send to alpha@astrometry.net the jobid on which source extraction did poorly. Also, please remember that you can always do the source extraction yourself and just send us a list of the X,Y (horizontal and vertical) positions of the sources within your image. The format is a simple FITS file which you upload to the web form. If you want an example of the format, you can download the FITS file of the sources as we extracted them.
Q: Is there a way for me to install the system locally on my own machine?
A: Not yet. But after we incorporate the feedback, improvements, and bug-fixes we get during the alpha testing phase (that's you!) we will release a complete source package and the data files necessary to reproduce a complete local installation. We use such local installations for our own science so we totally understand how useful they can be. Please email us and let us know you are interested and we'll notify you when the code comes out.
Q: Speaking of releasing code, when will you release the code? I want to look at the source!
A: Yeah, right. Sure you do. But seriously, we will be releasing code, right after the alpha test. Email code@astrometry.net and we'll put you on the list. If you are super-keen you can browse the source off of our trac pages: trac.astrometry.net
