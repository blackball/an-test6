Welcome Alpha Users

We are very excited that you are helping us test our service.
Below are some answers to commonly asked questions, which we'll try to update during the alpha testing period.
Q: What kinds of images will solve?
A: We don't know! That's why you are testing the service. What we do know is that we have solved images that range in size from under 10 arcmin to over 80 deg. We also know that we have solved images taken in bandpasses ranging from the near ultraviolet to the near infrared.
Q: Can I do precision proper-motion analyses with your output?
A: Right now we don't do extremely precise, proper-motion-grade astrometric solutions; these require performing simultaneous analyses of multiple images from the same instrument and simultaneous analyses of multiple images of the same part of the sky. This is on the long-term to-do list, but it ain't happening during the alpha phase. If you want precision, multi-image astrometry, we currently recommend taking the output of astrometry.net and using it as the input for SCAMP.
Q: I submitted an image and it didn't solve even though it had an almost exactly correct WCS header. How can you guys suck so badly that you can't even solve an image when I give you the right answer?
A: Currently, astrometry.net has to "verify" any solution by ensuring that your field and our all-sky index have a substantial number of sources in almost exact correspondence under our proposed alignment solution. The current astrometry.net will use the WCS information, if it is there, as an inital guess, but the "verification" of whether we think we have solved your image proceeds the same way regardless if we solved you image completely blind or using WCS as an initial guess. If your image contains very few sources which are known to our index, we are not able to verify the solution at high confidence and so we do not return a match. But please let us know what jobid failed, and what band your image was in, so we can improve our index to cover it.
Q: My image didn't solve even though I put in a correct estimate of the scale and I can see that your source extraction correctly extracted many USNO-B stars.
A: Can you phrase that as a question? We find that the most common error is to put in the scale as 25 but then accidentally pull down the tab "size of the image in degrees" when you meant "size of the image in arcmin". If you are sure that you have entered everything correctly and that source extraction is sane, and it still isn't solving, send email to "alpha@astrometry.net" with your jobid, because you clearly have a useful image for debugging.
Q: Why does my submission die silently without returning a jobid?
A: Did your job exceed the 250M size limit? If so, try gzip (yes, we take gz files). If not, there is a bug; send email to "alpha@astrometry.net".
Q: What does the system do with multi-extension files?
A: It just looks at the first image-like extension.
Q: I have tons of images I want to solve. Typing my email and the size parameters and the image urls into your lame web form over and over again is driving me crazy. Aren't you guys real scientists? Can't you give me a high-throughput option?
A: In the future there will be a command line client as well as the option for you to install the entire system locally at your site. For now, you can speed things up by submitting your first image manually using the web form and then hitting the LinkToTheseParameters button. That will construct a url which pre-fills the form. From then on, all you'll need to change is the url or local filename of the image. If that's still not good enough, the best we can offer you for now is the following hack, which uses wget. You need to expose your image on the web and fill in the info in the first three lines, ie your email/name, the url to your image and the size info (which you can just copy from the LinkToTheseParameters link). wget "http://live.astrometry.net/index.php?justjobid&email=user@domain.edu&uname=YourName&xysrc=url&imgurl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.astro.domain.edu%2Fuser%2Foutbox%2Fmyfield.jpg&fstype=ev&fse=1&fsv=50&fsunit=degreewidth&fsu=&fsl=&x_col=X&y_col=Y&parity=2&poserr=1&tweak=1&tweak_order=2&index=auto&submit=Submit" -Ojobid_out
Q: I looked at the source extraction preview image on the status page for one of my jobs, and your system totally failed to find tons of perfectly obvious sources in my image. What's up?
A: We're using a really *dumbass* source extraction algorithm for now, since it basically has to Just Work (tm) most of the time. We'd like to make it more sophisticated, so please send to "alpha@astrometry.net" the jobid on which source extraction did poorly.
Q: Is there a way for me to install the system locally on my own machine?
A: Not yet. But after we incorporate the feedback, improvements, and bug-fixes we get during the alpha testing phase (that's you!) we will release a complete source package and the data files necessary to reproduce a complete local installation. We use such local installations for our own science so we totally understand how useful they can be. Please email us and let us know you are interested and we'll notify you when the code comes out.
Q: Speaking of releasing code, when will you release the code? I want to look at the source!
A: Yeah, right. Sure you do. But seriously, we will be releasing code, right after the alpha test. Email code@astrometry.net and we'll put you on the list. If you are super-keen you can browse the source off of our trac pages: trac.astrometry.net
